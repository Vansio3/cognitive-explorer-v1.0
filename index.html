What Are Cognitive Biases?

Cognitive biases are systematic patterns of deviation from norm or rationality in judgment. They are mental shortcuts (heuristics) that help us make sense of the world quickly, but can sometimes lead to errors in thinking.

Understanding these biases helps us make more objective decisions and recognize potential pitfalls in our own reasoning and the reasoning of others.

Why Learn?

Improve decision-making in personal & professional life.

How They Form

Result from information processing shortcuts, motivations, social pressure.

Mitigation

Awareness, seeking feedback, and structured thinking are key.

Reflection Questions:

Possible Biases at Play:

  • Confirmation Bias: Favoring information aligning with existing beliefs.
  • Authority Bias: Trusting "Verified" or "Expert" sources more readily.
  • Bandwagon Effect: Being influenced by high like/comment counts.
  • Anchoring: Being influenced by the initial information (e.g., high price).
  • Optimism/Pessimism Bias: How you interpret the news or detox claims.
  • Survivorship Bias: Seeing only the success story (detox), not potential failures.
